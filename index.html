Attractions in the Area

It can be a long day of just waiting around the day of the show. To pass the time there are various attractions to keep you entertained or maybe calm the nerves. Here are some of the most popular:

  • The Mall in Washington DC: Museums with art, history, and science all on the same strip of land. Some muesuems are free, but double check their websites to confirm. My personal favorite is the Natural History Museum!
  • The International Spy Museum: It recently was relocated and revamped. The museum has hands on activities and provides a brief overview of spy agencies across the world.
  • The Wharf: The Anthem is located here. Surrounding the venue are various restaurants and shops. There are swings on the docks that make for a great place to take pictures!
  • The Mall in Columbia: This mall is five minutes from Merriweather Post Pavillion and has various shops and quick eats. I have stopped by here to pick up last minute items for my concert outfits.

Essential Items for the Show

Over the past couple of years, I've had the chance to go to a lot of concerts. With that, I have began to piece together a bag that carries all of my essentials for the show. Bag policies vary from show to show, so my first recommendation is to get a small clear bag. In my concert bag as of right now, I have:

  • Clear fanny pack
  • Portable charger
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Marker
  • Paper
  • Candy
